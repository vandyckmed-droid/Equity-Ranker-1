Patch the universe definition to optimize for actual retail usability, not domicile.

Goal:
Build the stock universe around what a normal US retail investor can easily buy and sell on a mainstream brokerage app with normal stock treatment, normal tax reporting, and no unusual fee/custody/structure complexity.

Important:
Do NOT use “US stocks only” as a rule.
That is too restrictive and wrongly excludes names I want, such as liquid US-traded foreign companies like TSM.

New universe standard:
Include any stock that is:
1. easily tradable on major US retail broker platforms,
2. sufficiently liquid for practical entry/exit,
3. subject to normal retail tax complexity only,
4. a true operating company equity rather than a fund or special vehicle.

Implementation rules

INCLUDE:
- US-listed common stocks
- US-listed ADRs / ADSs
- US-listed foreign operating companies that trade like normal stocks on mainstream retail platforms
- Mid-cap and large-cap names by default
- Companies outside the US are fine if they are US-traded, liquid, and operationally simple for a normal retail investor

EXCLUDE:
- OTC / Pink Sheet securities
- ordinary shares that require foreign-market access rather than normal US retail trading
- ETFs, CEFs, mutual funds
- SPACs, shell companies, acquisition vehicles
- preferred shares, warrants, rights, units
- LPs / MLPs / partnerships / structures likely to create K-1 or comparable non-standard tax handling
- BDCs
- leveraged / inverse products
- illiquid microcaps / small caps that are not realistically retail-friendly
- anything with obvious special trading, custody, fee, or tax complexity beyond normal stock ownership

Do NOT exclude solely because:
- headquarters are outside the US
- the company is foreign
- the security is an ADR / ADS
- there may be ordinary foreign dividend withholding that is still within normal retail investing complexity

Selection logic:
Prioritize practical retail usability over geography.
The universe should answer this question:
“Can a normal US retail investor buy and sell this easily, cheaply, and normally in a standard brokerage account?”

Suggested baseline filter:
- listing on NYSE or Nasdaq
- common stock or ADR/ADS only
- market cap focused on mid and large cap
- sufficient liquidity for routine trading

Refactor the universe layer so that it uses tradability-based inclusion, not nationality-based inclusion.
